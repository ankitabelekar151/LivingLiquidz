FAQ's

To preserve the quality of your wine, store it in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight and temperature fluctuations. Ideally, wines should be stored horizontally to keep the cork moist and prevent oxidation.

Scotch whisky is typically made from malted barley in Scotland and aged for at least three years. Bourbon, on the other hand, is predominantly made from corn and aged in charred oak barrels in the United States, giving it a sweeter and smoother profile compared to Scotch.

While most vodkas are gluten-free due to the distillation process, flavored vodkas may contain additives that could potentially introduce gluten. It's advisable to check the label or contact the manufacturer for specific gluten-free certifications.

When pairing beer with food, consider the flavors and intensities of both the dish and the beer. Light lagers and pale ales complement lighter dishes like salads and seafood, while stouts and porters pair well with hearty dishes such as grilled meats and chocolate desserts.
